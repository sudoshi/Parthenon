## Abby Textbook Keep Set

Active medical textbook ingestion is intentionally limited to methods-oriented titles that improve OHDSI retrieval quality without flooding the index with broad reference material.

Keep:
- `Epidemiology and Biostatistics: An Introduction to Clinical Research`
- `Biostatistical Methods in Epidemiology`
- `Applied Longitudinal Data Analysis`
- `Principles of Biostatistics`
- `Clinical Trials: Study Design, Endpoints and Biomarkers`
- `Cochrane Handbook for Systematic Reviews of Interventions`
- `Park's Textbook of Preventive and Social Medicine`
- `The Cell: A Molecular Approach`
- `Molecular Cell Biology`
- `Principles of Genetics`
- `Molecular Biology of the Gene`
- `Lewin's GENES XII`

Rejected categories:
- anatomy atlases and Netter collections
- surgery and procedural references
- broad clinical medicine textbooks already covered by other sources
- pathology, physiology, microbiology, virology, and additional molecular biology titles outside the curated core
- physics, math, and general science texts
- healthcare operations and patient-flow books

Rationale:
- Abby already has OHDSI source material and a separate clinical reference collection.
- The kept set adds study-design, epidemiology, longitudinal analysis, and evidence-synthesis context that is directly useful for OHDSI-style question answering.
- The added cell/genetics set gives Abby enough molecular background to reason about genes, variants, and nomenclature without turning the index into a general pre-med library.
- The rejected set is more likely to dilute retrieval than improve it.
