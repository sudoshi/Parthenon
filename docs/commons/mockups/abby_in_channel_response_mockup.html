Good question. You've used washout periods in three prior studies on this network:

1. SGLT2 Outcomes Study — Dr. Torres used a 180-day washout for SGLT2i exposure. In the review thread from January, she noted that 90 days was initially considered but rejected because refill gaps in claims data created false washout periods. The team settled on 180 days after checking refill patterns.

2. CKD Progression Study — 90-day washout was used for ACE inhibitor exposure and worked well. However, that cohort used pharmacy dispensing records (more precise) rather than claims, so refill gap issues were less relevant.

3. HTN Phenotyping — No washout was used; the team decided persistent exposure was the better design for that question.

Given that the GLP-1 RA cohort is likely built on claims data, the SGLT2 team's experience suggests 90 days may be too short. I'd recommend reviewing the refill gap distribution first — Dr. Udoshi's data quality checks in the DQ dashboard can show you the typical gap between GLP-1 RA fills.
